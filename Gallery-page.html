Girl with a Pearl Earring" is a well-known oil painting by Johannes Vermeer, created around 1665, and is one of his most celebrated works. It's an example of the "tronie" genre, depicting an imaginary figure rather than a portrait of a specific person. The painting is known for its subtle use of light and color, particularly on the girl's face and the pearl earring, which is a significant feature of the work.

The Mona Lisa is a half-length portrait painting by Leonardo da Vinci, considered an archetypal masterpiece of the Italian Renaissance. It depicts Lisa del Giocondo, the wife of Florentine merchant Francesco del Giocondo. The painting is known for its enigmatic smile, its use of sfumato (a "smoky" effect created by subtle gradations of light and shadow), and its enigmatic subject.

The Scream is an art composition created by Norwegian artist Edvard Munch in 1893. The Norwegian name of the piece is Skrik (Scream), and the German title under which it was first exhibited is Der Schrei der Natur (The Scream of Nature). The agonized face in the painting has become one of the most iconic images in art, seen as symbolizing the anxiety of the human condition. Munch's work, including The Scream, had a formative influence on the Expressionist movement.
